Mood & Direction

Atmosphere: A basement record store that also happens to serve the best espresso on the block. Exposed brick, a tattoo flash sheet framed on the wall, someone's vinyl set bleeding through the ceiling. You don't rush here.

Palette logic: Near-black as the total ground. Copper and rust as the only warm light — like a single Edison bulb over the bar. Everything else recedes.

Green treatment: The darkest interpretation — monstera as shadow, almost black-green. Appears only in plant tags and botanical moments, never as a background field.

Type: Syne 800 — the heaviest, most distinctive of the three options. High x-height, strong verticals. Echoes the boldness of tattoo lettering and vintage poster type. DM Sans light for body keeps it readable.

Sheet music / tattoo reference: Flash-sheet grid (faint crosshatch) baked into surfaces. Musical notes as near-invisible watermark. The angular button radius and badge shape pull from stamp/flash aesthetics. A "flash sheet" component for the menu — items laid out like tattoo designs on a card.
